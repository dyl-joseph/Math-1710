# 3.5 Curve Sketching

> [!info] Section 3.5 — Summary of Curve Sketching
> Covered on Mar 3 and Mar 5. Stewart Calculus 9e, Section 3.5.

## Overview

Curve sketching brings together everything from Chapters 1–3: limits, continuity, derivatives, asymptotes, extrema, concavity. The goal is to produce an accurate sketch of $y = f(x)$ by analyzing its features systematically.

See [[Curve Sketching Checklist]] for the full procedure with additional worked examples.

## The Checklist (Summary)

| Step | What to Find | Tool |
|---|---|---|
| A | Domain | Algebra |
| B | Intercepts | Set $x=0$, $y=0$ |
| C | Symmetry | Check $f(-x)$ |
| D | Asymptotes | Limits at $\pm\infty$, denominator zeros |
| E | Increasing/Decreasing | Sign of $f'$ |
| F | Local extrema | First/Second Derivative Test |
| G | Concavity, inflection | Sign of $f''$ |
| H | Sketch | Plot and connect |

## Worked Examples

> [!example] Example 1 — Rational Function
> Sketch $f(x) = \frac{2x^2}{x^2 - 1}$.
>
> **Domain:** $x \neq \pm 1$
>
> **Intercepts:** $f(0) = 0$. $x$-intercept at $x = 0$.
>
> **Symmetry:** $f(-x) = f(x)$ — **even**.
>
> **Asymptotes:**
> - VA: $x = 1$ and $x = -1$
> - HA: $y = 2$ (equal degrees, $\frac{2}{1}$)
>
> **First derivative:**
> $$f'(x) = \frac{4x(x^2-1) - 2x^2(2x)}{(x^2-1)^2} = \frac{-4x}{(x^2-1)^2}$$
>
> $f'(x) = 0$: $x = 0$. Sign chart:
>
> | Interval | $f'$ |
> |---|---|
> | $(-\infty, -1)$ | $+$ (increasing) |
> | $(-1, 0)$ | $+$ (increasing) |
> | $(0, 1)$ | $-$ (decreasing) |
> | $(1, \infty)$ | $-$ (decreasing) |
>
> Local max at $(0, 0)$.
>
> **Behavior near VAs:**
> - As $x \to 1^+$: $f(x) \to +\infty$. As $x \to 1^-$: $f(x) \to -\infty$.
> - Symmetric at $x = -1$.
>
> **Sketch:** Even function with local max at origin, VAs at $\pm 1$, approaching $y = 2$ at both ends.

> [!example] Example 2 — Polynomial
> Sketch $f(x) = x^4 - 6x^2 + 4$.
>
> **Domain:** All $\mathbb{R}$. **Symmetry:** Even.
>
> **Intercepts:** $f(0) = 4$.
>
> **End behavior:** $\lim_{x \to \pm\infty} f(x) = +\infty$
>
> **First derivative:**
> $f'(x) = 4x^3 - 12x = 4x(x^2 - 3) = 4x(x-\sqrt{3})(x+\sqrt{3})$
>
> Critical numbers: $x = 0, \pm\sqrt{3}$
>
> | Interval | $f'$ | Behavior |
> |---|---|---|
> | $(-\infty, -\sqrt{3})$ | $-$ | Decreasing |
> | $(-\sqrt{3}, 0)$ | $+$ | Increasing |
> | $(0, \sqrt{3})$ | $-$ | Decreasing |
> | $(\sqrt{3}, \infty)$ | $+$ | Increasing |
>
> Local min at $x = \pm\sqrt{3}$: $f(\sqrt{3}) = 9 - 18 + 4 = -5$
>
> Local max at $x = 0$: $f(0) = 4$
>
> **Second derivative:** $f''(x) = 12x^2 - 12 = 12(x^2-1)$
>
> Concave up on $(-\infty, -1) \cup (1, \infty)$, concave down on $(-1, 1)$.
>
> Inflection points: $(\pm 1, -1)$.
>
> **Sketch:** W-shape. Local max at $(0, 4)$, local mins at $(\pm\sqrt{3}, -5)$, inflection at $(\pm 1, -1)$.

> [!example] Example 3 — With Slant Asymptote
> Sketch $f(x) = \frac{x^2 - 2x}{x - 3}$.
>
> **Domain:** $x \neq 3$
>
> **Intercepts:** $f(0) = 0$. $x$-intercepts: $x(x-2) = 0 \implies x = 0, 2$.
>
> **Asymptotes:**
> - VA: $x = 3$
> - Long division: $\frac{x^2-2x}{x-3} = x + 1 + \frac{3}{x-3}$. Slant asymptote: $y = x + 1$.
>
> **First derivative:**
> $$f'(x) = \frac{(2x-2)(x-3) - (x^2-2x)(1)}{(x-3)^2} = \frac{x^2 - 6x + 6}{(x-3)^2}$$
>
> $f'(x) = 0$: $x = 3 \pm \sqrt{3}$, i.e., $x \approx 1.27$ and $x \approx 4.73$.
>
> $f(3-\sqrt{3}) = 2 - 2\sqrt{3} \approx -1.46$ — local max
>
> $f(3+\sqrt{3}) = 2 + 2\sqrt{3} \approx 5.46$ — local min
>
> **Sketch:** Crosses $x$-axis at $0$ and $2$, local max near $(1.27, -1.46)$, VA at $x = 3$, local min near $(4.73, 5.46)$, approaches slant asymptote $y = x+1$.

> [!example] Example 4 — Function with $e^x$
> Sketch $f(x) = x^2 e^{-x}$.
>
> **Domain:** All $\mathbb{R}$. $f(x) \ge 0$ for all $x$.
>
> **Intercepts:** $f(0) = 0$.
>
> **End behavior:** $\lim_{x \to \infty} x^2e^{-x} = 0$ (exponential wins). $\lim_{x \to -\infty} x^2e^{-x} = \infty$.
>
> HA: $y = 0$ (right side only).
>
> **First derivative:**
> $f'(x) = 2xe^{-x} - x^2e^{-x} = xe^{-x}(2-x)$
>
> $f'(x) = 0$: $x = 0, 2$
>
> | Interval | $f'$ | Behavior |
> |---|---|---|
> | $(-\infty, 0)$ | $-$ | Decreasing |
> | $(0, 2)$ | $+$ | Increasing |
> | $(2, \infty)$ | $-$ | Decreasing |
>
> Local min at $(0, 0)$. Local max at $(2, 4e^{-2}) \approx (2, 0.54)$.
>
> **Second derivative:**
> $f''(x) = e^{-x}(x^2 - 4x + 2) = 0 \implies x = 2 \pm \sqrt{2}$
>
> Inflection points at $x \approx 0.59$ and $x \approx 3.41$.

## Tips

> [!tip] Exam Strategy
> - **Start with the easiest steps:** domain, intercepts, symmetry. These are fast points.
> - **Asymptotes before derivatives:** knowing end behavior helps you check your derivative work.
> - **Sign charts are essential.** Organize them clearly — this is what the grader looks for.
> - **Label everything** on your sketch: extrema with coordinates, asymptotes as dashed lines, inflection points.
> - **Professor Allaart expects complete solutions** — show all work, not just the graph.
