# 3.3 The Shape of Graphs

> [!info] Section 3.3 — What Derivatives Tell Us About the Shape of a Graph
> Covered on Feb 24 and Feb 26. Stewart Calculus 9e, Section 3.3.

## Overview

The first derivative tells us where a function is increasing/decreasing and locates local extrema. The second derivative tells us about concavity and inflection points. Together, they give a complete picture of a function's shape.

## What $f'$ Tells Us

> [!thm] Increasing/Decreasing Test
> - $f'(x) > 0$ on $I$ $\implies$ $f$ is **increasing** on $I$
> - $f'(x) < 0$ on $I$ $\implies$ $f$ is **decreasing** on $I$

**Procedure:** Find critical numbers, make a sign chart for $f'$, determine intervals of increase/decrease.

See [[First and Second Derivative Tests]] for the First Derivative Test for local extrema.

> [!example] Example 1 — Increasing/Decreasing and Local Extrema
> $f(x) = 2x^3 + 3x^2 - 36x + 5$
>
> $f'(x) = 6x^2 + 6x - 36 = 6(x^2 + x - 6) = 6(x+3)(x-2)$
>
> Critical numbers: $x = -3, 2$
>
> | Interval | $f'$ sign | Behavior |
> |---|---|---|
> | $(-\infty, -3)$ | $+$ | Increasing |
> | $(-3, 2)$ | $-$ | Decreasing |
> | $(2, \infty)$ | $+$ | Increasing |
>
> - Local max at $x = -3$: $f(-3) = -54 + 27 + 108 + 5 = 86$
> - Local min at $x = 2$: $f(2) = 16 + 12 - 72 + 5 = -39$

## What $f''$ Tells Us

> [!thm] Concavity Test
> - $f''(x) > 0$ on $I$ $\implies$ $f$ is **concave up** on $I$
> - $f''(x) < 0$ on $I$ $\implies$ $f$ is **concave down** on $I$

An **inflection point** is where concavity changes. See [[Concavity and Inflection Points]] for details.

> [!example] Example 2 — Concavity and Inflection Points
> Continuing Example 1: $f(x) = 2x^3 + 3x^2 - 36x + 5$
>
> $f''(x) = 12x + 6 = 0 \implies x = -\frac{1}{2}$
>
> | Interval | $f''$ sign | Concavity |
> |---|---|---|
> | $(-\infty, -\frac{1}{2})$ | $-$ | Down |
> | $(-\frac{1}{2}, \infty)$ | $+$ | Up |
>
> Inflection point at $\left(-\frac{1}{2}, f(-\frac{1}{2})\right) = \left(-\frac{1}{2}, 23.5\right)$

## The Second Derivative Test

> [!thm] Second Derivative Test
> If $f'(c) = 0$:
> - $f''(c) > 0$ → **local min** (concave up)
> - $f''(c) < 0$ → **local max** (concave down)
> - $f''(c) = 0$ → **inconclusive** (use First Derivative Test)

> [!example] Example 3 — Second Derivative Test
> $f(x) = xe^{-x}$
>
> $f'(x) = e^{-x} - xe^{-x} = e^{-x}(1-x)$. Critical number: $x = 1$.
>
> $f''(x) = -e^{-x}(1-x) + e^{-x}(-1) = e^{-x}(x-2)$
>
> $f''(1) = e^{-1}(1-2) = -e^{-1} < 0$ → **local max** at $(1, e^{-1})$
>
> Inflection: $f''(x) = 0$ at $x = 2$. Sign changes from $-$ to $+$. Inflection at $(2, 2e^{-2})$.

## Complete Graph Analysis

> [!example] Example 4 — Full Analysis
> $f(x) = x^4 - 4x^3$
>
> **First derivative:**
> $f'(x) = 4x^3 - 12x^2 = 4x^2(x - 3)$
>
> Critical numbers: $x = 0, 3$
>
> | Interval | $f'$ sign | Behavior |
> |---|---|---|
> | $(-\infty, 0)$ | $-$ | Decreasing |
> | $(0, 3)$ | $-$ | Decreasing |
> | $(3, \infty)$ | $+$ | Increasing |
>
> No extremum at $x = 0$ (no sign change). Local min at $x = 3$: $f(3) = 81 - 108 = -27$.
>
> **Second derivative:**
> $f''(x) = 12x^2 - 24x = 12x(x-2)$
>
> | Interval | $f''$ sign | Concavity |
> |---|---|---|
> | $(-\infty, 0)$ | $+$ | Up |
> | $(0, 2)$ | $-$ | Down |
> | $(2, \infty)$ | $+$ | Up |
>
> Inflection points: $(0, 0)$ and $(2, -16)$.
>
> **Summary:**
> - Decreasing on $(-\infty, 3)$, increasing on $(3, \infty)$
> - Local min: $(3, -27)$. No local max.
> - Concave up on $(-\infty, 0) \cup (2, \infty)$, concave down on $(0, 2)$
> - Inflection points: $(0, 0)$ and $(2, -16)$

> [!example] Example 5 — Trig Function
> $f(x) = \sin x + \cos x$ on $[0, 2\pi]$
>
> $f'(x) = \cos x - \sin x = 0 \implies x = \frac{\pi}{4}, \frac{5\pi}{4}$
>
> $f''(x) = -\sin x - \cos x$
>
> $f''\!\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right) = -\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} - \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} = -\sqrt{2} < 0$ → **local max**, $f\!\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right) = \sqrt{2}$
>
> $f''\!\left(\frac{5\pi}{4}\right) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} + \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} = \sqrt{2} > 0$ → **local min**, $f\!\left(\frac{5\pi}{4}\right) = -\sqrt{2}$
>
> Inflection: $f''(x) = 0 \implies \tan x = -1 \implies x = \frac{3\pi}{4}, \frac{7\pi}{4}$

## Tips

> [!tip] Key Points
> - **Sign chart for $f'$:** determines increasing/decreasing and local extrema
> - **Sign chart for $f''$:** determines concavity and inflection points
> - **First Derivative Test is more reliable** — always gives an answer. Use Second Derivative Test when $f''$ is easy to compute.
> - **Inflection point requires sign change in $f''$** — $f''(c) = 0$ alone is not enough.
> - The shape of $f$ near a critical point: concave up + horizontal tangent = local min, concave down + horizontal tangent = local max.
