# 2.7 Rates of Change in the Sciences

> [!info] Section 2.7 — Rates of Change in the Natural and Social Sciences
> Covered on Feb 5. Stewart Calculus 9e, Section 2.7.

## Overview

The derivative $f'(x)$ is the instantaneous rate of change of $f$ with respect to $x$. This section applies that idea across physics, biology, chemistry, and economics. The math is the same — only the interpretation changes.

See [[Applications of Derivatives to Science]] for expanded treatment of each domain.

## Physics: Motion Along a Line

> [!def] Position, Velocity, Acceleration
> For a particle with position $s(t)$:
> - **Velocity:** $v(t) = s'(t)$
> - **Speed:** $|v(t)|$
> - **Acceleration:** $a(t) = v'(t) = s''(t)$

Key questions:
- **Moving forward/backward?** Check sign of $v(t)$
- **Speeding up/slowing down?** Check if $v(t)$ and $a(t)$ have same or opposite signs

> [!example] Example 1 — Particle Motion
> A particle moves along a line with $s(t) = t^3 - 6t^2 + 9t + 2$ (meters, seconds).
>
> $v(t) = 3t^2 - 12t + 9 = 3(t-1)(t-3)$
>
> $a(t) = 6t - 12 = 6(t-2)$
>
> | Interval | $v(t)$ | $a(t)$ | Motion |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | $0 < t < 1$ | $+$ | $-$ | Forward, slowing down |
> | $1 < t < 2$ | $-$ | $-$ | Backward, speeding up |
> | $2 < t < 3$ | $-$ | $+$ | Backward, slowing down |
> | $t > 3$ | $+$ | $+$ | Forward, speeding up |

## Physics: Other Rates

> [!example] Example 2 — Linear Density
> A wire has mass $m(x) = x^2 + 3x$ grams, where $x$ is meters from the left end.
>
> Linear density: $\rho(x) = m'(x) = 2x + 3$ g/m
>
> At $x = 2$: $\rho = 7$ g/m. The wire gets denser as you move right.

## Biology: Population Growth

> [!example] Example 3 — Population Growth Rate
> A population of bacteria is $P(t) = 10{,}000 + 500t - 2t^2$ after $t$ hours.
>
> Growth rate: $\frac{dP}{dt} = 500 - 4t$
>
> At $t = 50$: $\frac{dP}{dt} = 300$ bacteria/hr
>
> Growth stops at $t = 125$ hr (population peaks at $P(125) = 41{,}250$).

## Chemistry: Reaction Rates

> [!example] Example 4 — Concentration
> A reactant has concentration $C(t) = \frac{5}{2t + 1}$ mol/L.
>
> Rate of change: $C'(t) = \frac{-10}{(2t+1)^2}$ mol/(L·min)
>
> At $t = 2$: $C'(2) = \frac{-10}{25} = -0.4$ mol/(L·min). Concentration is decreasing at 0.4 mol/L per minute.

## Economics: Marginal Cost and Revenue

> [!def] Marginal Functions
> - **Marginal cost** $C'(x)$: approximate cost of producing one more unit
> - **Marginal revenue** $R'(x)$: approximate revenue from selling one more unit
> - **Profit maximized** when $R'(x) = C'(x)$

> [!example] Example 5 — Marginal Analysis
> Cost: $C(x) = 2000 + 8x + 0.01x^2$. Revenue: $R(x) = 20x - 0.002x^2$.
>
> $C'(x) = 8 + 0.02x$, $R'(x) = 20 - 0.004x$
>
> Profit maximized when $8 + 0.02x = 20 - 0.004x$, giving $x \approx 500$ units.
>
> At $x = 500$: marginal cost $= \$18$/unit, marginal revenue $= \$18$/unit.

## Tips

> [!tip] Key Reminders
> - **Always include units** in your answer. The units of $f'(x)$ are (units of $f$) / (units of $x$).
> - **Read carefully:** "how fast is ___ changing" = find a derivative. "When is ___ at a maximum" = set derivative to zero.
> - **Negative rates** mean decrease. Don't drop the sign — it carries meaning.
> - **Speed vs velocity:** speed is always non-negative ($|v(t)|$), velocity can be negative.
