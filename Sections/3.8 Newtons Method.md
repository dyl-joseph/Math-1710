# 3.8 Newton's Method

> [!info] Section 3.8 — Newton's Method
> Covered on Mar 19. Stewart Calculus 9e, Section 3.8.

## Overview

Newton's method is an iterative algorithm for approximating roots of equations ($f(x) = 0$). It uses tangent lines to rapidly converge to a solution.

## The Idea

Start with an initial guess $x_1$ near a root. The tangent line at $(x_1, f(x_1))$ crosses the $x$-axis at a point $x_2$ that is (usually) closer to the root. Repeat.

## The Formula

> [!thm] Newton's Method
> Starting from an initial guess $x_1$, generate successive approximations:
> $$x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)}$$

### Derivation

The tangent line at $(x_n, f(x_n))$ is:
$$y - f(x_n) = f'(x_n)(x - x_n)$$

Set $y = 0$ and solve for $x$:
$$0 - f(x_n) = f'(x_n)(x - x_n) \implies x = x_n - \frac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)}$$

## Worked Examples

> [!example] Example 1 — $\sqrt{2}$ via Newton's Method
> Find $\sqrt{2}$ by solving $f(x) = x^2 - 2 = 0$.
>
> $f'(x) = 2x$. Formula: $x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{x_n^2 - 2}{2x_n} = \frac{x_n^2 + 2}{2x_n} = \frac{1}{2}\left(x_n + \frac{2}{x_n}\right)$
>
> Starting with $x_1 = 1$:
>
> | $n$ | $x_n$ | $f(x_n)$ |
> |---|---|---|
> | 1 | $1$ | $-1$ |
> | 2 | $1.5$ | $0.25$ |
> | 3 | $1.41\overline{6}$ | $0.00694$ |
> | 4 | $1.41421568...$ | $0.0000060$ |
> | 5 | $1.41421356...$ | $\approx 0$ |
>
> After just 5 iterations, we have 8 decimal places of accuracy.

> [!example] Example 2 — Solve $x^3 + x - 1 = 0$
> $f(x) = x^3 + x - 1$, $f'(x) = 3x^2 + 1$.
>
> $f(0) = -1 < 0$, $f(1) = 1 > 0$. Root in $(0, 1)$ by IVT.
>
> Start with $x_1 = 0.5$:
>
> $x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{x_n^3 + x_n - 1}{3x_n^2 + 1}$
>
> | $n$ | $x_n$ | $f(x_n)$ |
> |---|---|---|
> | 1 | $0.5$ | $-0.375$ |
> | 2 | $0.7143$ | $0.0787$ |
> | 3 | $0.6824$ | $0.0006$ |
> | 4 | $0.6823$ | $\approx 0$ |
>
> Root $\approx 0.6823$.

> [!example] Example 3 — Finding Where Two Curves Intersect
> Find where $\cos x = x$ (i.e., solve $f(x) = \cos x - x = 0$).
>
> $f'(x) = -\sin x - 1$. Start with $x_1 = 0.5$:
>
> $x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{\cos x_n - x_n}{-\sin x_n - 1}$
>
> | $n$ | $x_n$ |
> |---|---|
> | 1 | $0.5$ |
> | 2 | $0.7553$ |
> | 3 | $0.7391$ |
> | 4 | $0.7391$ |
>
> Solution: $x \approx 0.7391$.

> [!example] Example 4 — Solve $e^x = 3 - 2x$
> Rewrite: $f(x) = e^x + 2x - 3 = 0$. $f'(x) = e^x + 2$.
>
> $f(0) = -2 < 0$, $f(1) = e - 1 \approx 1.72 > 0$. Root in $(0,1)$.
>
> Start $x_1 = 0.5$:
>
> | $n$ | $x_n$ |
> |---|---|
> | 1 | $0.5$ |
> | 2 | $0.5344$ |
> | 3 | $0.5340$ |
>
> Root $\approx 0.5340$.

## When Newton's Method Fails

> [!tip] Potential Problems
> 1. **$f'(x_n) = 0$:** Division by zero. The tangent is horizontal and doesn't cross the $x$-axis. Choose a different starting point.
> 2. **Cycling:** The iterates can cycle between values without converging. Example: $f(x) = x^{1/3}$ near $x = 0$.
> 3. **Divergence:** Bad initial guess can send iterates away from the root. Always start near a root (use IVT to locate one first).
> 4. **Converging to wrong root:** If there are multiple roots, the method may converge to one you didn't want.

## Convergence

> [!tip] Rate of Convergence
> When Newton's method works, it converges **quadratically** — the number of correct decimal places roughly **doubles** with each step. This makes it extremely fast.
>
> Compare: the bisection method (from IVT) is much slower — it only gains about one decimal place every 3–4 steps.

## Tips

> [!tip] Practical Tips
> - **Always use IVT first** to confirm a root exists and locate an interval. Use the midpoint as $x_1$.
> - **Show a table** of iterations on exams. Include $x_n$ and $f(x_n)$ columns.
> - **Stop when $f(x_n)$ is close to 0** or when successive $x_n$ values agree to the desired precision.
> - **Calculators aren't allowed** on exams, so Professor Allaart will likely give problems where 2–3 iterations are manageable by hand, or ask you to set up the formula without computing.
